Track Introduction

Colorado Road is a Grand Prix-type race track that is very long. Situated somewhere southwest of Denver, Colorado, USA; this track utilizes a lot of natural terrain. It also has recently been involved in more eco-friendly practices. Windmills power parts of the circuit outside of its base power source. This course has a lot of speedy sections but also a lot of elevation changes and technical corners. This course will challenge drivers of different levels ranging from amateur to professionals.

Three layouts are on offer for all who race at Colorado Road. No matter what configuration a driver uses or a race is held on, a number of challenges will await all drivers of all skill levels. The Junior layout can fun for those looking for some thrill in going up elevation changes. The National and Grand Prix Layouts will offer up the Colorado Carousel- a sweeping long right with banking on the inside and a quick kink that goes downhill upon exit. For those who challenge the Grand Prix course, prepare to experience some jumps going uphill. The Grand Prix course has a speedy uphill section, but one had better be ready to slow down hard once the Grand Prix course reaches its highest elevation point.

The challenge and length of Colorado Road also lends itself to endurance racing possibilities. An amateur-level endurance can be utilized on the National course, and drivers ranging from high-end amateurs to world-class racing drivers can take on the full Grand Prix layout for an endurance. The National and Grand Prix layouts can suit up to 40 cars.


Junior Layout Discussion

The Junior Layout of Colorado Road utilizes the eastern end of the course. It is almost essentially a modified oval with six corners and a kink. It is meant to be for up to 20 cars. This track mostly suits low-powered to mid-powered sports cars.


National Layout Discussion

The National Layout of the course uses the Grand Prix Circuit, but only the first third of the course. The first two corners are fairly sharp. The first elevation change leads to the Colorado Carousel. The uphill entry is fairly blind, so use proper judgment to determine the best line. It is also a good idea to avoid going too wide in this area. Once the Carousel is cleared, a quick drop in elevation change leads up to the next sharp corner. The course then goes downhill and to the left. Gentle corners make up the shorter course. Make sure your car is set up to take on the switchbacks that lead to the Start/Finish straight.


Grand Prix Layout Discussion

The ultimate challenge of Colorado Road is its Grand Prix course. While the other two layouts have one set of elevation changes, the Grand Prix layout has three. After the downhill of the National course, the Grand Prix course goes right. A straight follows into the Junior layout and the uphill trudge and jump. After clearing the Junior section, go straight ahead into the uphill section with two jumps. Towards the slow chicane, it is mostly narrow to remind you to slow down considerably. Clearing the chicane leads to a softer chicane that leads to a soft downhill section. Going downhill completely leads to the pit road and the kink and corner leading to the Start-Finish line. This layout is very long, so be ready to endure an intense racing challenge.
