Gaucho Pants


DESCRIPTION:

Gaucho pants are culotte-type pants that were very trendy in the mid-2000s.  These pants are usually made of stretchy material and have skirt-like legs.  They can be thought of as riding skirts.  They are also essentially cropped palazzo pants.  Gaucho pants usually extend as far downward as just past the knees, but some even go down to about mid-calf.  Gaucho pants are ridiculously comfortable that they can be worn as lounge clothing as much as they can be worn outside of the house.  These pants were meant initially to be worn with tall boots.  However, any variety of sneakers, pumps, or sandals can compliment these all-year pants.  These split skirts are comfortable and stylish to wear.  Gaucho pants would be the precursor to a future trend- yoga pants.  Gaucho pants, like yoga pants, have been notorious for one big reason- making butts look big and providing discomforting views of a femaleâ€™s backside.  Some who wear gaucho pants think their butts look cute wearing them, and they feel comfortable in them.  So while gaucho pants may no longer be trendy, they are not forgotten in being comfortable and stylish.


THOUGHTS:

I have always loved gaucho pants.  Those who want to wear skirt-like pants or some stylish culottes can do no wrong with gaucho pants.  Dress up gaucho pants with anything ranging from simple flip-flop/thong sandals to more chic footwear.  You could even wear some daytime shoes with gaucho pants for a day, then switch to some more classy and chic shoes for night wear with gaucho pants.  They are surely versatile.  If you fear your backside looking big wearing gaucho pants, then you may want to avoid these pants.
